Wentworth Falls

Lincolns Rock Lookout

Lincolns rock is a lookout point in Wentworth falls which looks out into the Jamison Valley in Blue Mountains National Park. The lookout is named after Lincoln Hall, an Australian Mountaineer who was a long time resident of Wentworth Falls.

If you venture down below the rock lookout (via a trail located to the left of the lookout) you will be led down to the base of a small elevated cave. This cave can be accessed via a small rock climb and is the perfect spot for a picnic.

The lookout does not have banisters so if you are accompanied by children, be aware of the height risk.

Distance: 20m from carpark
Parking: Car park available
Toilet: Not available

Wentworth Falls Track

The Wentworth falls track is a peaceful walk from the Wentworth Falls lookout down to the Falls. The track is well signposted and maintained but is very steep.

The track leads to a shallow pool mid-way down the falls, this section of the waterfall sits 100m above the spectacular Jamison Valley which is visible throughout the walk.

Once reaching the falls there is the option to turn back and return the way you came or continue on to the National Pass track which leads to some more secluded but equally breathe-taking lookouts.

Distance: 1.4km return
Parking: Car park available
Toilet: Available at the carpark
