Blackheath

Govett's Leap Lookout

Govett’s Leap Lookout provides visitors with an incredible view of the Grose Valley and is recognised as one of the most popular lookouts in the Blue Mountains (after Echo Point). The lookout showcases the tall cliffs and deep canyons of the Blue Mountains National park. The lookout is equipped with picnic tables and is a perfect spot for a lunch stop.

This lookout is the beginning of the Govett’s Leap Decent, which leads down to the base of the 180m waterfall that can be observed from the lookout.

From the lookout, areas damaged from the 2019/2020 Australian bushfires can be seen.

Parking: Car park available
Toilet: Available

Grand Canyon Track

The Grand Canyon walk starts at Evans lookout and loops down into Grose Valley before returning back to the starting point. The walk leads hikers down through a variety of different terrains, ranging from the open Australian bush to dark dense areas of foliage by the river bed.

This walk is very scenic and is home to an abundance of waterfalls and river crossings. The well-maintained track facilitates easy movement through more dense and slippery areas. The Australian bush is home to many Australian creatures that can be spotted along the way with some luck.
This walk will take an estimated three to four hours, so be prepared with adequate food and water.

Distance: 5.4km Loop
Parking: Car park available
Toilet: Not available
