What are the Blue Mountains?

The Blue Mountains National Park is an area of nearly 270,000 hectares that is comprised of tall cliffs and deep canyons within the Australian Bush. The National Park is a tourist hub in Australia, providing activities for people of all ages and abilities.


Choose from the Blue Mountains suburbs: Wentworth Falls, Leura, Katoomba and Blackheath in the menu bar to see the main attractions.

Attractions

Bushwalks Lookouts Waterfalls

Bush Walks

The Blue Mountains is home to some of Australia's most picturesque bushwalks through the Australian bush. Each bush walk varies in length and difficulty, meaning there is a walk for people of all abilities.

Lookouts

Each lookout in the Blue Mountains allows visitors to peer into the beautiful National park landscape. The lookout invites visitors to view the sandstone cliffs and deep canyons that have been created by waterways over thousands of years.

Waterfalls

Waterfalls are found all around the Blue Mountains. Many waterfalls make great picnic or swimming spots for visitors to get the full Blue Mountain experience.

What to Bring

The Blue Mountains is home to the full range of weather conditions that can change very quickly throughout the day. In summer it can reach above thirty degrees and in winter it can drop down below zero degrees. The type of activities visitors engage in can lead them into secluded locations without access to water or shelter.

To best prepare for your trip to the Blue Mountains, remember to bring:

  • Water Bottle (1 litre minimum)

  • Rain protection

  • Warm Jacket (Even in summer)

  • Sun Protection (hat, sunscreen)

Where are the Blue Mountains?

The Blue Mountains National Park is located 80km west of the Sydney CDB. The park is accessable via car travel or public transport.
